## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**Abbott's July 17 Ratepayer Memo Deadline Has Passed — No Confirmation Yet That PUC/ERCOT Actually Filed It**

- Friday, July 17 was Gov. Abbott's deadline for the PUC and ERCOT to submit their joint memorandum identifying additional ratepayer-protection actions and any statutory limitations. As of this morning, no public release of that memo or confirmation it was filed has surfaced — it's still an open compliance question rather than a resolved one.
- The next Abbott deadline is now the closer one to watch: the PUC must initiate action to reduce residential ratepayers' transmission costs by July 31.
- No new ERCOT news release is dated today; the most recent on ERCOT's site remains the June 18 release on PUCT's approval of the Batch Zero large-load process.
- Batch Zero mechanics are unchanged: the process took effect July 11; TSPs/DSPs serving large-load projects (including DFW-area ones) must submit full technical packages to ERCOT by July 24, with classification notices due August 7. ERCOT is tracking more than 438,000 MW of large-load interconnection requests systemwide, with roughly 89% coming from data centers.

**Today's Signal:** With the July 17 memo deadline now past and unconfirmed, the next hard date is July 31 — the PUC's own deadline to act on transmission-cost relief for residential ratepayers.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**BlackRock/ACS's New Coravel Platform Lands a DFW Campus Deal — Plug Power/Stream Sale Still on Track for July 31**

- BlackRock and ACS formally launched Coravel, their new 50/50 data center development platform, on July 15 — and announced an undisclosed hyperscaler has already committed to 140 MW of critical IT load across three purpose-built facilities at a Dallas-Fort Worth campus. Turner Construction will lead delivery, with service dates staggered through 2028.
- The Plug Power sale of its Graham, Texas site (Young County, just northwest of the DFW metro) — land plus 164 MW of grid interconnection assets — to Stream Data Centers remains on track to close on or about July 31, for up to $76.5 million plus roughly $14 million in released cash collateral.
- Outside the DFW footprint, two large Panhandle/West Texas campuses were announced this week: QTS Data Centers and Lancium unveiled a 1 GW, 465-acre, 11-building campus in Hall County (July 18, over $10 billion in projected investment), and Crusoe and Lancium announced a second 1 GW campus in Childress County, extending their existing Abilene partnership. Neither is in the core DFW counties, but both add to the statewide buildout pace industry trackers are citing.
- The Fort Worth Report published a map (July 16) of the city's existing and proposed data centers, underscoring how concentrated recent siting activity has become around the DFW core even as the zoning fight over new projects continues (see Item 4).

**Today's Signal:** The Coravel/BlackRock-ACS entry into the DFW market is the most concrete new construction signal this week — a fresh capital platform, not just an existing hyperscaler, now has boots on the ground locally.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**EPA's Minor-Source Permit Hearing Is Two Days Out — No New DFW-County Filing Today**

- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties surfaced today.
- The EPA's proposed rollback of public-participation requirements for "minor source" air permits — the category covering many diesel generators and gas turbines powering Texas data centers — remains the dominant storyline. The registration deadline for the EPA's virtual public hearing was Sunday, July 19; the hearing itself runs July 22, 9 a.m.–3 p.m. CT; written comments stay open through August 21.
- TCEQ has still not said publicly whether it would scale back its own public-participation rules if the EPA's rollback is finalized.
- Recurring statewide figures: at least 38 Texas data centers have received minor-source permits for on-site power since 2024, enabling more than 2,100 backup diesel generators statewide; the Vantage data center near San Antonio (80-plus diesel generators) remains the most-cited example.

**Today's Signal:** The July 22 EPA hearing is now the nearest concrete date — the last live opportunity for Texans to weigh in before any rule change reshapes how much notice communities get on future gas- and diesel-generator permits.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**A National Protest Wave Hit Texas Harder Than Any Other State This Weekend**

- The "Humans First" National Day of Data Center Protests took place Saturday, July 18, with 142 events across 42 states. Texas hosted 18 of those events — more than any other state — including one in Lubbock where Agriculture Commissioner Sid Miller spoke against data center development.
- Fort Worth's zoning fight now has a firmer date: after the Zoning Commission's 7-4 denial of the city's proposed data-center ordinance (July 8) sent it back to City Council, council is expected to take it up August 11, when members return from summer break. Five council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) remain on record supporting a temporary moratorium in the meantime; Mayor Parker and three others were not asked to sign on.
- Denton's organizing push (town hall held July 16) continues to point toward an August council decision on a possible local moratorium or ban; no vote has been scheduled yet.
- No new DFW-area county rezoning filing or vote surfaced dated specifically to today. Earlier land-use flashpoints remain unresolved in the background: Parker County's pushback over Black Mountain's 2,000-plus-acre purchase for a Fort Worth-area project, and Hood County's conditional approval of the 2,100-acre "Comanche Circle" proposal near Glen Rose.

**Today's Signal:** The weekend's protest turnout — Texas leading the nation in event count — is the clearest sign yet that local land-use opposition is now a statewide political force heading into Fort Worth's and Denton's August council dates.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**No New DFW Deal Today — Equipment Bottlenecks Keep Pushing Operators Off-Grid**

- No new DFW-specific on-site generation or gas+battery hybrid announcement surfaced today.
- Reporting this week underscores why "power-first" strategies keep spreading: switchgear is reportedly sold out through 2028 and substation transformer lead times have stretched to roughly 160 weeks and are still rising, a bottleneck industry trackers say the DFW core can't absorb on existing infrastructure alone.
- The Chevron-Microsoft 20-year power purchase agreement for a dedicated natural-gas plant near Pecos (roughly 2.5 GW initially, ~$7 billion, targeting 2027) remains the marquee "power-first" deal statewide, alongside Pacifico Energy's GW Ranch project in Pecos County — reportedly the largest air permit ever granted in the U.S. at 7.65 GW.
- The pending EPA minor-source rollback (Item 3) would directly ease the permitting path for the diesel and gas behind-the-meter fleets DFW-region data centers already rely on to satisfy Senate Bill 6's backup-generation requirement for 75 MW-plus facilities.

**Today's Signal:** With switchgear and transformer lead times stretching past 2028, expect more Texas operators to follow the Pecos/Permian model of building dedicated generation rather than waiting in ERCOT's queue — a trend the DFW market is increasingly exposed to even without a local deal this week.
