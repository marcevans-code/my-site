## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**Batch Zero Deadline Just Passed • Queue Still Near 438 GW**

- The July 10, 2026 deadline for large-load projects to submit technical studies and documentation for ERCOT's "Batch Zero" interconnection process has now passed. Industry estimates put roughly 55 large-load projects totaling 21.7 GW in position for inclusion.
- ERCOT is still tracking more than 438 GW of large-load interconnection requests systemwide, with nearly 90% of that demand coming from data centers.
- Next milestone: ERCOT is expected to notify Batch Zero applicants of their classification (base load, studied load, or excluded) around August 7, 2026.
- The full system-wide steady-state and stability screening study, which will set Year 1–6 MW allocations, isn't expected until April 2027, with a final transmission plan covering the batch due in fall 2027.

**Today's Signal:** With the Batch Zero submission window now closed, watch for any early word — official or leaked — on which DFW-area projects made the cut ahead of the August 7 classification notice.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**Denton Numbers Surface Ahead of Town Hall • Hyperscalers Keep Filing**

- Denton's Core Scientific and QumulusAI data centers, both slated to finish this fall, are contracted for enough grid capacity to power roughly 8.5 DFW International Airports, 495 H-E-Bs, or 82,000 homes — notably more than Denton's own 57,000 households.
- AWS has acquired roughly 1,300 acres near Cedar Creek in Bastrop County and separately filed for a $1.2 billion, ~2,700-acre campus in Wharton County.
- Meta has released plans for a $1.5 billion AI data center in El Paso — its third data center project in the state.
- Google's previously announced $40 billion Texas AI/cloud infrastructure commitment continues to be cited by both supporters and critics of the buildout.

**Today's Signal:** Denton's load-versus-household comparison is exactly the kind of stat likely to dominate Thursday's town hall and any DFW coverage this week — worth tracking for pickup by other outlets.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**No New DFW-County Filing Today — But Cumulative Emissions Report Keeps Circulating**

- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties turned up today.
- A statewide accounting published in the past two weeks identified 32 proposed gas-fired power plants tied to Texas AI data centers, projected to collectively emit more than 287 million tons of greenhouse gases annually, plus thousands of tons of particulate matter and nitrogen oxides.
- The DFW region's existing 10-county ozone nonattainment designation (Collin, Dallas, Denton, Ellis, Johnson, Kaufman, Parker, Rockwall, Tarrant, Wise) remains the regulatory backdrop against which any new gas-plant permit near DFW will be evaluated.

**Today's Signal:** Nothing new to flag today for DFW-specific permits — treat this as a quiet day on the TCEQ front, but the statewide emissions report continues to be cited in local reporting and could reignite county-level scrutiny at any time.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**Fort Worth Zoning Fight Paused for Summer • Denton Grassroots Push Building**

- Fort Worth's Zoning Commission voted 7-4 on July 8 to deny the city's proposed data-center zoning ordinance (which would have required 250-foot setbacks from homes and banned crypto mining as a primary use), sending it back to City Council.
- On July 10, a five-member majority of Fort Worth's City Council (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) publicly called for a temporary moratorium on new data-center development, to be formally proposed when Council returns from summer break in August. Council is now expected to take up the zoning ordinance August 4, with a final vote as early as August 11.
- Two large Fort Worth rezonings already on the books: Black Mountain's 450 acres in southeast Fort Worth (agricultural to industrial, for a proposed $10 billion data-center project) and Edged Data Centers' 186 acres in Veale Ranch, southwest Fort Worth.
- In Denton, DSA Denton and the Revolutionary Front announced (July 13) a town hall for Thursday, July 16 at Emily Fowler Central Library to organize around a possible local moratorium or ban, citing the Core Scientific/QumulusAI load figures above.

**Today's Signal:** Both of DFW's biggest local-control fights — Fort Worth's council vote and Denton's grassroots organizing — are on hold or building toward action in the next 1–4 weeks. Nothing to act on today, but circle August 4/11 (Fort Worth) and July 16 (Denton).

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**Texas Holds Roughly Half of the Nation's Tracked Behind-the-Meter Gas Projects**

- A national tally of 74 proposed behind-the-meter gas plants serving data centers found Texas accounts for nearly half of them.
- OpenAI's Stargate campus in Abilene (1,100 acres) includes a permitted 360 MW on-site gas plant with 10 turbines allowed to emit up to 1.6 million tons of greenhouse gases and 1,000 tons of air pollutants per year.
- Chevron is reportedly negotiating its first-ever dedicated power plant, sized at 5 GW, to serve a behind-the-meter AI data-center complex in the Permian Basin.
- Circe Energy has secured 2 GW of natural gas capacity for a West Texas data-center campus.

**Today's Signal:** The Chevron Permian Basin talks are the one to watch — a 5 GW dedicated plant from a major oil major, if finalized, would be one of the largest single behind-the-meter commitments reported in Texas to date.
