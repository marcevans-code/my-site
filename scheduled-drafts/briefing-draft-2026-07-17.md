## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**Today Is the Deadline for PUC/ERCOT's Ratepayer-Protection Memo to Abbott**

- Today, July 17, is the deadline Gov. Greg Abbott set in his June 10 directive letter for the PUC and ERCOT to submit a joint memorandum to his office identifying additional actions under their existing authority to safeguard residential and small-business ratepayers from data-center-driven infrastructure costs, along with any statutory limitations and legislative recommendations. No public confirmation that the memo was actually filed, or its contents, had surfaced as of this morning — it's a compliance deadline to watch rather than a released document.
- A second Abbott deadline follows close behind: the PUC must initiate action to reduce residential ratepayers' transmission costs by July 31.
- No new ERCOT news release is dated today; the most recent on ERCOT's site remains the June 18 release announcing PUCT's approval of the Batch Zero large-load interconnection process.
- The Batch Zero calendar is unchanged: TSPs/DSPs serving large-load projects (including DFW-area ones) must submit full technical packages by July 24, with ERCOT classification notices due August 7 and a final deficiency-cure deadline of August 31.

**Today's Signal:** Today's real test is whether PUC and ERCOT actually deliver Abbott's ratepayer-safeguard memo on schedule — a compliance deadline worth checking for a same-day release, since none had surfaced as of this morning.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**No New DFW-Specific Filing Today — Plug Power/Stream Deal Still on Track for July 31**

- No new dated construction, land-acquisition, or interconnection filing specific to the core DFW counties (Tarrant, Denton, Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Wise, Parker, Hood) surfaced today.
- The Plug Power sale of its Graham, Texas site — land plus 164 MW of grid interconnection assets — to Stream Data Centers remains on track to close on or about July 31, for up to $76.5 million ($50 million at closing, up to $26.5 million tied to final load capacity), plus release of roughly $14 million in cash collateral. Graham sits in Young County, just northwest of the core DFW metro.
- Outside the DFW footprint, Pacifico Energy's GW Ranch project in Pecos County (West Texas) continues to be cited industry-wide as a template: a TCEQ-permitted 7.65 GW off-grid gas-fired campus purpose-built for a hyperscale data center, with first power targeted for Q1 2027.
- Statewide backdrop unchanged: Texas has at least 248 planned data center projects, with industry trackers describing DFW construction as "in hyperdrive" and projecting the under-construction pipeline alone will double the DFW market's size by the end of 2026.

**Today's Signal:** With no new DFW-specific corporate filing today, the July 31 close of the Plug Power/Stream deal remains the nearest concrete date to watch.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**No New DFW-County Filing Today — Advocacy Pressure Still Building Ahead of July 19/22 EPA Dates**

- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties surfaced today.
- The EPA's proposed rollback of "minor source" air-permit public-participation requirements — the category covering many diesel generators and gas turbines powering Texas data centers — remains the dominant storyline, carried over from the Texas Tribune's July 16 report. The registration deadline for the EPA's virtual public hearing is Saturday, July 19; the hearing itself is July 22; written comments remain open through August 21.
- TCEQ did not respond to the Tribune's requests for comment on whether it would move to scale back its own public-participation rules if the EPA's rollback is finalized.
- Recurring statewide figures: at least 38 Texas data centers have received minor-source permits for on-site power since 2024, enabling more than 2,100 backup diesel generators; the Vantage data center near San Antonio remains the cited example, permitted to emit 99.8 tons per year of nitrogen oxides.

**Today's Signal:** No new filing today, but the July 19 hearing-registration deadline is now just two days out — the last real window for Texans to secure a speaking slot before the July 22 EPA hearing.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**Denton's Organizing Town Hall Happened Last Night — No Vote, Council Action Still Pointed at August**

- DSA Denton and the Revolutionary Front held their town hall at Emily Fowler Central Library at 6:30 p.m. last night, July 16, to organize residents around a possible local data-center moratorium or ban. It was a community organizing event, not a city council vote; Denton's council is not expected to take up a moratorium until August, per Mayor Pro Tem Nick Stevens.
- Fort Worth's timeline is unchanged from prior updates: the Zoning Commission's 7-4 denial of the city's proposed data-center ordinance (July 8) sent it back to City Council, which is expected to take it up after returning from summer break in August; five council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) remain on record supporting a temporary moratorium in the meantime.
- No new DFW-area county rezoning filing or vote surfaced dated specifically to today.
- Statewide backdrop: a Texas Tribune poll (June 23) found most Texans oppose data centers in their own communities, with opposition most intense in rural counties — the same dynamic playing out in Denton and Fort Worth.

**Today's Signal:** Both of this week's marquee DFW land-use fights — Denton's organizing push and Fort Worth's zoning fight — are now paused until their respective August council dates, leaving little new to report at the local level today.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**No New DFW Deal Today — Statewide "Bring Your Own Power" Trend Keeps Building**

- No new DFW-specific on-site generation or gas+battery hybrid announcement surfaced today.
- The Chevron-Microsoft 20-year power purchase agreement for a dedicated natural-gas plant near Pecos in the Permian Basin (roughly 2.5 GW initially, ramping toward 2.67-5 GW, ~$7 billion, targeting 2027) remains the marquee "power-first" deal industry-wide; it sits outside the DFW footprint but continues to be cited as the model other Texas operators are watching, alongside Pacifico Energy's GW Ranch project (Item 2).
- The pending EPA minor-source rollback (Item 3) would directly ease the permitting path for the diesel and gas behind-the-meter fleets that DFW-region data centers already rely on to satisfy Senate Bill 6's requirement that facilities with 75 MW-plus demand carry backup power generation.
- Statewide, behind-the-meter gas investment tied to data centers is estimated near 38 GW in development, per industry trackers, as operators route around ERCOT's congested interconnection queue.

**Today's Signal:** No new DFW-specific deal today, but the throughline between the EPA's permitting rollback (Item 3) and the broader ~38 GW behind-the-meter buildout keeps sharpening as the industry's default power strategy.
