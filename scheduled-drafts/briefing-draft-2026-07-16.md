## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**Batch Zero Window Narrows Toward the July 24 TSP Deadline**

- Today, July 16, falls in the gap between two Batch Zero milestones: the July 15 deadline for large-load projects to show development progress, and the July 24 deadline for transmission/distribution service providers (TSPs/DSPs) to submit full technical packages — Load Information Form, Dynamic Stability Study Form, and related attestations — to ERCOT.
- No new ERCOT news release is dated today; the most recent remains the June 18 release announcing PUCT's approval of the Batch Zero process itself.
- Queue math is unchanged: ERCOT's large-load interconnection queue remains near 438-439 GW systemwide, with roughly 89% of that demand attributed to data centers — about one-third of all U.S. generating capacity and roughly five times Texas's current peak demand record.
- Independent analysts estimate around 55 large-load projects totaling 21.7 GW are positioned to make the Batch Zero cohort; ERCOT's classification notices to developers are due by August 7, with a final deficiency-cure deadline of August 31.

**Today's Signal:** With the July 15 progress-showing behind them, pressure now shifts to TSPs/DSPs serving DFW-area large-load projects to finish and file technical packages before the July 24 cutoff.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**No New DFW-Specific Filing Today — Plug Power/Stream Deal Still on Track for July 31**

- No new dated construction, land-acquisition, or interconnection filing specific to the core DFW counties (Tarrant, Denton, Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Wise, Parker, Hood) surfaced today.
- The Plug Power sale of its Graham, Texas site — land plus 164 MW of grid interconnection assets — to Stream Data Centers remains on track to close on or about July 31, for up to $76.5 million. Graham sits in Young County, just northwest of the core DFW metro.
- Statewide backdrop: industry trackers describe DFW data center construction as being "in hyperdrive," with under-construction capacity alone projected to double the size of the DFW market by the end of 2026. Texas is projected to overtake Virginia as the nation's largest data center hub by 2030, with roughly 300 operating facilities statewide and 200-plus more in development.
- Fermi America's Project Matador near Amarillo remains a story of stalled momentum: the company says an "initial phase" of construction is complete, but satellite tracking has not shown building starts, with the company citing a pending final Clean Air Permit ruling as the hold-up.

**Today's Signal:** With no new DFW-specific corporate filing today, the July 31 close of the Plug Power/Stream deal remains the nearest concrete date to watch.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**Fresh Reporting Today Adds Advocacy Voices to the EPA Minor-Source Rollback Fight**

- New today: the Texas Tribune published fresh reporting (July 16) on the EPA's proposed rule change for "minor source" air permits — the category covering many of the diesel generators and gas turbines that power Texas data centers. The story adds on-record objections from Air Alliance Houston's Jennifer Hadayia, Public Citizen's Kathryn Guerra, Sierra Club Lone Star's Cyrus Reed, and Fenceline Watch's Yvette Arellano, all warning that TCEQ — already criticized as a reluctant regulator — could use new EPA flexibility to scale back public-comment rights on these permits.
- Confirmed timeline: the EPA proposal (published in the Federal Register July 7) has a virtual public hearing set for July 22 (registration due July 19), with written comments accepted through August 21.
- Recurring figures cited again today: at least 38 Texas data centers have received minor-source permits for on-site power since 2024, enabling more than 2,100 backup diesel generators statewide. The Vantage data center near San Antonio is cited as an example, permitted to emit 99.8 tons per year of nitrogen oxides.
- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties surfaced today.

**Today's Signal:** The new on-record objections from four separate advocacy groups in today's Tribune story signal organized opposition is building ahead of the July 19 registration deadline and July 22 EPA hearing.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**Denton Town Hall Tonight — Fort Worth's Public Comment Window Just Closed**

- Tonight is the marquee date: DSA Denton and the Revolutionary Front hold a town hall at 6:30 p.m. today, July 16, at Emily Fowler Central Library, to organize Denton residents around a possible local data-center moratorium or ban. Organizers cite a regional count of 86 planned data centers in North Texas, 56 in Central Texas, and 45 in West Texas.
- Fort Worth's public comment window on its proposed data-center zoning ordinance closed yesterday, July 15. City staff will fold that feedback into an August 4 work session with City Council, followed by an August 11 vote on whether to begin the state-required legal process for a temporary moratorium (a final council vote on any moratorium isn't expected until December; state law caps moratoriums at 90 days plus one 90-day extension).
- Five Fort Worth council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) remain on record supporting that moratorium push, which followed the Zoning Commission's 7-4 denial of the underlying ordinance on July 8.
- No new DFW-area county rezoning filing or vote surfaced dated specifically to today.

**Today's Signal:** Tonight's Denton town hall is the one live, in-person organizing event on today's calendar — watch for whether it produces the kind of council-member moratorium pledge that followed Fort Worth's zoning vote.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**No New DFW Deal Today — Permian Chevron-Microsoft Plant Still the Template**

- No new DFW-specific on-site generation or gas+battery hybrid announcement surfaced today.
- The Chevron-Microsoft 20-year power purchase agreement for a dedicated natural-gas plant near Pecos in the Permian Basin — sized at roughly 2.5 GW initially, ramping toward 2.67-5 GW, at an estimated $7 billion, targeting 2027 operation — remains the marquee "power-first" deal in the pipeline. It sits outside the DFW footprint but continues to be cited as the model other Texas hyperscale operators are watching.
- The pending EPA minor-source rollback (Item 3) would directly ease the permitting path for the diesel and gas behind-the-meter fleets that data centers across Texas, including several in the DFW region, already rely on to satisfy Senate Bill 6's requirement that facilities with 75 MW-plus demand carry backup power generation.
- Statewide, behind-the-meter gas investment tied to data centers is estimated near 38 GW in development, per industry trackers, as operators route around ERCOT's congested interconnection queue.

**Today's Signal:** No new DFW-specific deal today, but the throughline between the EPA's permitting rollback (Item 3) and the broader ~38 GW behind-the-meter buildout keeps sharpening as the industry's default power strategy.
