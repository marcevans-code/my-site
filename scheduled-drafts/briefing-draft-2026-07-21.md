## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**Three Days to the Batch Zero Technical-Package Deadline — Abbott's July 17 Memo Still Unconfirmed**

- No new ERCOT news release is dated today; the most recent on ERCOT's site remains the June 18 release on PUCT's approval of the Batch Zero large-load interconnection process.
- The Batch Zero clock keeps advancing: TSPs/DSPs serving large-load projects (including DFW-area ones) must submit full technical packages to ERCOT by this Friday, July 24, with classification notices due back to interconnecting large loads by August 7. ERCOT's systemwide large-load queue remains north of 438,000 MW, roughly 89% of it tied to data centers.
- Gov. Abbott's July 17 deadline for the PUC and ERCOT to jointly submit a ratepayer-safeguard memo (summarizing existing protections, statutory limits, and legislative recommendations) has now passed by four days with no public release or confirmation of filing surfacing in coverage today.
- The nearer-term Abbott deadline to watch is now just 10 days out: the PUC must initiate action to reduce residential ratepayers' transmission costs by July 31.

**Today's Signal:** With the Abbott memo still unconfirmed, the July 24 Batch Zero technical-package deadline is the next hard, verifiable milestone — worth checking ERCOT's filings once the week closes.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**Hedgehog USA Signs Idemitsu to Fuel a New 600 MW "Powered Land" Campus in Navarro County**

- Hedgehog USA announced today (July 21) a long-term energy supply agreement with Japan's Idemitsu to power a planned data center campus in Navarro County — just south of the DFW metro along the I-45 corridor — engineered to support up to 600 MW of capacity by the end of 2027. The site is designed to run entirely on natural gas burned on-site, bypassing ERCOT's interconnection queue altogether (see Item 5).
- No new dated announcement today from the core DFW hyperscaler/PE names (Meta, AWS, Google, Microsoft) or from BlackRock/ACS's Coravel platform, whose 140 MW DFW campus commitment (announced July 15) remains the most concrete recent construction signal inside the metro itself.
- The Plug Power sale of its Graham, Texas site (Young County, just northwest of DFW) — land plus 164 MW of grid interconnection assets — to Stream Data Centers remains on track to close on or about July 31, for up to $76.5 million plus roughly $14 million in released cash collateral.
- Statewide context continues to build around DFW's edges: recent weeks brought a 1 GW QTS/Lancium campus in Hall County and a second 1 GW Crusoe/Lancium campus in Childress County, both extending the Abilene-model buildout pattern into new counties.

**Today's Signal:** Hedgehog/Idemitsu's Navarro County deal is today's clearest new construction signal — another operator choosing to build its own gas plant rather than wait on ERCOT, in a county immediately adjacent to the DFW data-center corridor.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**One Day Out From the EPA's Minor-Source Permitting Hearing — No New DFW-County Filing Today**

- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties surfaced today.
- The EPA's proposed rollback of public-participation requirements for "minor source" air permits — the category covering many of the diesel generators and gas turbines powering Texas data centers — remains the dominant storyline. The agency's virtual public hearing is tomorrow, July 22, 9 a.m.–3 p.m. CT; the registration window closed July 19, but written comments stay open through August 21.
- Recurring statewide figures still being cited in coverage: at least 38 Texas data centers have received minor-source permits for on-site power since 2024, enabling more than 2,100 backup diesel generators statewide.
- Separately, Pacifico Energy's GW Ranch project in Pecos County remains the largest air permit ever granted in the U.S. — 7.65 GW of gas-fired generation for hyperscale data centers — a scale advocates point to as evidence TCEQ's minor-source review process is being outpaced by the industry it's meant to regulate.

**Today's Signal:** Tomorrow's EPA hearing is the last live public forum before any rule change reshapes how much notice DFW-area communities get on future gas- and diesel-generator permits — worth watching for turnout and TCEQ's own reaction.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**Fort Worth and Denton Both Still Pointing to August Council Dates — Nothing New Filed Today**

- No new DFW-area county rezoning filing or vote surfaced dated specifically to today.
- Fort Worth's zoning fight remains set for August 11, when City Council reconvenes from summer break to take up the data-center ordinance the Zoning Commission denied 7-4 on July 8. Five council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) plus Michael Crain are on record backing a temporary moratorium in the meantime; Mayor Parker and three others were not asked to sign on.
- Denton's organizing push — a DSA Denton/Revolutionary Front town hall held July 16 — continues to point toward a possible council vote on a local moratorium or ban, with organizers citing that the city's Core Scientific and QumulusAI data centers, once complete this fall, could draw enough power for roughly 82,000 Denton homes. No formal council vote date has been set yet.
- Earlier land-use flashpoints remain unresolved in the background: Parker County's pushback over Black Mountain's 2,000-plus-acre purchase for a Fort Worth-area project, and Hood County's conditional approval of the 2,100-acre "Comanche Circle" proposal near Glen Rose.

**Today's Signal:** With both Fort Worth and Denton's decision points still three weeks out, today is a quiet news day on DFW land use — the next real movement to watch for is any early signal on how council votes might break in August.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**Hedgehog/Idemitsu's Navarro County Deal Is Today's Marquee "Skip the Queue" Story**

- Hedgehog USA's agreement with Idemitsu (Item 2) is the clearest power-first signal today: a 600 MW campus built from the ground up to run on natural gas burned on-site, explicitly designed to avoid the multi-year wait now facing large loads in ERCOT's interconnection queue.
- Equipment bottlenecks continue to be cited as the structural reason operators keep choosing this path: switchgear is reportedly sold out through 2028 and substation transformer lead times have stretched to roughly 160 weeks and rising — infrastructure constraints the DFW core can't absorb without new capacity.
- The Chevron-Microsoft 20-year power purchase agreement for a dedicated natural-gas plant near Pecos (roughly 2.5 GW initially, ~$7 billion, targeting 2027) remains the marquee statewide "power-first" deal, alongside Microsoft's separate ~2.67 GW co-located gas plant for Project Kilby near Pecos.
- The pending EPA minor-source rollback (Item 3) would directly ease the permitting path for the diesel and gas behind-the-meter fleets DFW-region data centers already rely on to satisfy Senate Bill 6's backup-generation requirement for facilities of 75 MW or more.

**Today's Signal:** Hedgehog/Idemitsu's Navarro County deal — announced today, just south of the DFW corridor — is the newest evidence that "power-first" siting has moved from West Texas into DFW's immediate periphery.
