## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals
**Batch Zero's Utility-Side Deadline Lands Today — The Nine-Month Interconnection Clock Starts Ticking for the First Wave**

- Today, July 24, is the deadline for transmission and distribution service providers to submit the utility-side half of the Batch Zero technical package to ERCOT — the second of two required filings, following applicant-side submissions that closed July 10.
- The PUC approved ERCOT's Batch Zero large-load connection process on June 18, 2026, creating a structured on-ramp for the roughly 410 GW of large-load interconnection requests ERCOT is tracking statewide, about 87% of which are data centers.
- Once both halves of a Batch Zero filing are complete, ERCOT's nine-month interconnection study clock begins — meaning today's deadline effectively starts the timer for the first cohort of large loads to move through the new process.
- Standing context: the PUC's July 9 vote (5-0) to adopt "ride-through" rules — requiring data centers and other large loads to stay connected through grid disturbances rather than trip offline — continues to draw pushback, with operators estimating compliance costs of $500,000 to $1 million per megawatt and hinting at litigation.

**Today's Signal:** Whatever gets filed today effectively sets the front of the queue for the state's new large-load interconnection process — worth watching in the coming weeks how many of the roughly 410 GW in pending requests actually clear this first procedural gate.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity
**BlackRock Formally Launches Its $12.3B Bond Sale for Meta's El Paso Campus**

- BlackRock began marketing a bond sale targeting more than $12.3 billion to help finance Meta's AI data-center campus in El Paso, with BlackRock's infrastructure arms taking roughly 80% ownership and Meta holding the remaining 20% — a structure designed to keep the debt off BlackRock's own balance sheet.
- The deal, arranged with major Wall Street banks and first reported last week by the Wall Street Journal and Bloomberg, is now moving to formal pricing; it ranks among the largest debt financings yet tied to a single U.S. data-center project.
- The El Paso campus draws on El Paso Electric's grid capacity and is expected to come online in 2028; Meta had already grown its committed investment there to roughly $10 billion as of March 2026.
- No new dated land purchase, interconnection filing, or air-permit application specific to the DFW metro surfaced today. JLL's 2026 market outlook still projects Texas overtaking Northern Virginia as the world's largest data-center market by 2030.

**Today's Signal:** The bond sale's growth from "more than $12 billion" last week to $12.3 billion in today's marketing suggests strong investor demand — worth watching whether final pricing confirms appetite for an off-balance-sheet structure this large, since it's likely to become the template for other Texas mega-campuses.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments
**No New Filing Today — Fort Worth and Denton Both Still Parked on August Dates**

- No new zoning filing or county-commissioner vote occurred today. Fort Worth's Zoning Commission voted 7-4 on July 8 to reject staff's proposed data-center standards (250-foot residential setbacks, generator siting limits, acoustic barriers for rooftop cooling equipment, and industrial-only zoning); five council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) are instead pushing a temporary moratorium, with City Council expected to take up both proposals August 11.
- City staff disclosed in a June presentation that data centers have generated more than $83 million in gross Fort Worth property-tax revenue over the past five years — a figure likely to shape the August 11 debate.
- No new rural land purchase, brownfield pivot, or industrial/warehouse filing masking data-center intent surfaced today for Parker, Hood, Wise, or the other outlying counties tracked in prior briefings.
- Statewide pressure continues to build: Gov. Abbott has named data-center regulation a priority for the 2027 legislative session and has asked PUC and ERCOT leadership to ensure data centers — not residential ratepayers — shoulder the cost of their own grid growth.

**Today's Signal:** With both Fort Worth and Denton parked on August council dates, the next real DFW zoning news is unlikely before the August 4–11 window — worth using the lull to track whether other North Texas counties file similar standards in the meantime.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation
**GE Vernova's Gas-Turbine Backlog Hits 116 GW as Grid Wait Times Push Operators Off-Grid**

- GE Vernova reported yesterday that its gas-turbine order backlog climbed to 116 GW, part of a total company order backlog of $176 billion — up from $129 billion in the same quarter last year — with CEO Scott Strazik saying the company is now taking turbine reservations for 2031 delivery.
- Coverage framed the backlog explicitly around AI infrastructure demand, with one report quoting the growing order book as evidence that "AI data centers cannot wait" for standard grid interconnection timelines — a dynamic directly fueling the shift toward behind-the-meter and on-site gas generation in Texas.
- Standing context: a July 2 study covered by KERA found Texas accounts for nearly half of the 74 proposed behind-the-meter gas plants tracked nationally, with potential combined emissions exceeding 287 million tons of greenhouse gases annually if all were built.
- Meta's El Paso campus continues to lean on more than 800 mobile mini-turbines for bridge power ahead of permanent on-site generation coming online.

**Today's Signal:** A multi-year turbine backlog now stretching to 2031 all but guarantees Texas operators will keep leaning on behind-the-meter and mobile generation as a bridge — worth watching whether that pushes more campuses toward the same modular-turbine approach Meta is using in El Paso.
