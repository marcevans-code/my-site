## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**EPA's Minor-Source Hearing Is Live Today — Batch Zero's Utility-Side Deadline Two Days Out, Queue Now North of 226 GW**

- The EPA holds its virtual public hearing today, July 22, 9 a.m.–3 p.m. CT, on the proposed rollback of public-notice requirements for "minor source" air permits — the category covering most data-center backup generators and gas turbines. Written comments stay open through August 21 (see Item 3).
- ERCOT's Large Load Working Group meets tomorrow, July 23, for its regular working session on Batch Zero implementation and large-load interconnection process changes.
- The Batch Zero utility-side technical package is due to ERCOT on July 24 (two days out); the applicant-side submissions were already due July 10, and any remaining deficiencies face a final cure deadline of August 31.
- PUCT formally approved ERCOT's Batch Zero process this summer, making ERCOT the first ISO in the country to batch-assess large-load interconnection requests rather than processing them one at a time — intended to keep new data-center load additions in line with what the grid can reliably support.
- Scale check: ERCOT's large-load interconnection queue has grown from roughly 63 GW a year ago to about 226 GW as of this year, with nearly all of the growth attributed to AI/computational load. Separately, PUCT is pushing legislative proposals to expand its own authority over data-center operators — including curtailment communication rules, new reporting requirements, and safeguards against grid disruption by foreign-linked operators.

**Today's Signal:** Two live threads converge today — the EPA hearing in the morning and, tomorrow, ERCOT's own working group digesting Batch Zero's two-days-out utility deadline. Worth watching both for how they interact with PUCT's push for more data-center-specific regulatory authority.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**AWS Files a $1.2B, 2,700-Acre Wharton County Campus — Chevron/Microsoft Talks Advance in West Texas**

- AWS has filed for a roughly $1.2 billion data center campus on about 2,700 acres near FM 1301/FM 442 in Wharton County (southwest of Houston, outside the DFW core but a significant statewide signal) — four buildings at ~189,000 sq ft each, with construction on all four set to start August 1. The first three are slated to finish by early January 2027, the fourth by August 2027.
- Microsoft and Chevron are reportedly in exclusive negotiations to co-locate a 2.5 GW gas-fired power hub with a new Microsoft AI campus in West Texas; GE Vernova turbines are already said to be on order, with a possible 2027 online date.
- Meta's $10 billion El Paso campus — grown sixfold from an original $1.5 billion commitment — continues targeting 1 GW of capacity by 2028; Meta is separately building out 366 MW of on-site modular gas generation there (see Item 5).
- Inside the DFW metro specifically, no new corporate filing surfaced dated to today; the most recent concrete regional signals remain Hedgehog USA's July 21 Idemitsu agreement (Navarro County, just south of DFW) and the pending Plug Power–Stream Data Centers Graham, Texas sale (Young County), still on track to close around July 31.

**Today's Signal:** The Wharton County and West Texas activity underscores that Texas's data-center land rush is broadening well beyond the DFW core — worth tracking whether that statewide capital flow starts pulling attention (and grid capacity debate) away from the Fort Worth/Denton fights.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**EPA Hearing Is the Headline Statewide Story — No New DFW-County Filing Surfaced Today**

- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties surfaced today.
- Today's EPA virtual hearing (9 a.m.–3 p.m. CT) is the dominant thread: the agency's proposal would strip public-notice and comment requirements from "minor source" permits nationally — the category most data-center diesel generators and gas turbines fall under. Advocacy groups (Sierra Club, CleanTechnica) have flagged the scale nationally: one investigation found roughly 10,500 diesel generators clustered at data centers in Northern Virginia alone, totaling nearly 27 GW.
- All eight named DFW-area counties (Collin, Cooke, Dallas, Denton, Ellis, Erath, Fannin, Grayson, Hood, Hunt, Johnson, Kaufman, Navarro, Palo Pinto, Parker, Rockwall, Somervell, Tarrant, Wise) fall under TCEQ Region 4, which is also affected by the DFW nine-county area's "serious" ozone nonattainment status (effective since July 2024, attainment deadline August 2027) — a designation that raises the compliance bar for any new gas-fired permit in the region.
- Recurring statewide figures still being cited in coverage: at least 38 Texas data centers have received minor-source permits for on-site power since 2024, enabling more than 2,100 backup diesel generators statewide.

**Today's Signal:** The DFW area's "serious" nonattainment status is worth flagging as a standing headwind for any new gas-fired data-center permit here — regardless of what the EPA's minor-source hearing decides nationally, DFW's air-quality designation already invites more scrutiny than most of the rest of the state.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**New Detail Surfaces on Fort Worth's August 11 Proposal — 250-Foot Residential Buffer, No Crypto Mining**

- Fort Worth city staff have drafted the specific new standards headed to the August 11 council vote: a mandatory 250-foot buffer between data-center buildings and residential areas, noise-mitigation requirements, closed-loop cooling system standards, and an outright prohibition on facilities dedicated to cryptocurrency mining. City staff have advised against a separate formal moratorium, arguing the new standards already address the concerns raised.
- The five council members on record backing a temporary moratorium (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) still plan to formally propose it at the August 11 meeting, setting up a direct staff-vs-council split to watch.
- Denton's push remains a step behind Fort Worth's: Mayor Pro Tem Nick Stevens and newly elected Mayor Chris Watts have both floated a council moratorium discussion, but no formal vote date is set. The July 16 town hall (hosted by DSA Denton and the Revolutionary Front) drew standing-room-only attendance; organizers noted Denton's Core Scientific and QumulusAI data centers, once complete this fall, will together draw enough ERCOT load to power roughly 8.5 DFW International Airports or 82,000 Denton homes.
- Earlier flashpoints remain unresolved in the background: Parker County's pushback over Black Mountain's 2,000-plus-acre purchase, and Hood County's conditional approval of the 2,100-acre "Comanche Circle" proposal near Glen Rose.

**Today's Signal:** The newly reported specifics of Fort Worth's August 11 proposal (250-ft buffer, no crypto mining, closed-loop cooling) give a much more concrete picture of what "regulation instead of moratorium" would actually look like — worth comparing against whatever Denton eventually proposes.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**Meta's El Paso Gas Array and a New Abilene-Area 400 MW JV Join Chevron/Microsoft's West Texas Plans**

- Meta is deploying 366 MW of modular natural-gas generation — 813 individual modular units — to power its 1 GW El Paso campus behind the meter, explicitly bypassing ERCOT's interconnection queue; the units are slated to come online by 2027.
- A new 400 MW behind-the-meter project has been announced in the greater Abilene area, a joint venture between PowerPlay AI and an unnamed Nasdaq-listed neocloud company.
- Cummins has agreed to supply natural-gas generator sets to Circe Energy for a scalable, behind-the-meter prime-power microgrid supporting a Texas high-performance-computing data center.
- Statewide, Texas is now estimated to have roughly 38 GW of behind-the-meter generation in development — the most of any state — out of an estimated 101 GW announced nationally, with more than 57 GW of that already having equipment on order and about 7 GW under construction.
- The Chevron-Microsoft West Texas talks (Item 2) and Microsoft's earlier ~2.67 GW co-located gas plant for Project Kilby remain the marquee statewide power-first deals. Equipment bottlenecks continue to be cited as the structural driver pushing operators toward on-site generation: switchgear reportedly sold out through 2028, and substation transformer lead times stretched to roughly 160 weeks.

**Today's Signal:** The Abilene JV and Meta's El Paso build-out show the behind-the-meter/power-first pattern spreading well past the handful of marquee deals — worth treating this as the default strategy for new large Texas campuses now, not the exception.
