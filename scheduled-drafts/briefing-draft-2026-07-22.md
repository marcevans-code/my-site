## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**EPA's Minor-Source Permitting Hearing Happens Today — Batch Zero Deadline Two Days Out**

- The EPA holds its virtual public hearing today, July 22, 9 a.m.–3 p.m. CT, on the proposed rollback of public-notice requirements for "minor source" air permits — the category covering most data-center backup generators and gas turbines. This is the last live public forum before any rule change takes effect; written comments stay open through August 21 (see Item 3).
- The Batch Zero technical-package deadline is now two days away: TSPs/DSPs serving large-load projects (including DFW-area ones) must submit full technical packages to ERCOT by Friday, July 24, with classification notices due back to interconnecting large loads by August 7.
- Gov. Abbott's July 17 deadline for the PUC and ERCOT to jointly submit a ratepayer-safeguard memo has now passed by five days with no public release or confirmation of filing surfacing in coverage today.
- The nearer-term Abbott deadline remains the PUC's July 31 obligation to act on reducing residential ratepayers' transmission costs — nine days out. ERCOT's systemwide large-load queue remains north of 438,000 MW, roughly 89% of it tied to data centers.

**Today's Signal:** Today's EPA hearing is the day's clearest live event — worth watching for turnout and any TCEQ reaction, since the outcome directly shapes future notice requirements for DFW-area gas and diesel permits.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**EPA Public Hearing Live Today — No New DFW-County TCEQ Filing**

- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties surfaced today.
- The dominant storyline is today's EPA virtual public hearing (9 a.m.–3 p.m. CT) on rolling back public-participation requirements for minor-source air permits — the category covering many of the diesel generators and gas turbines powering Texas data centers. Written comments remain open through August 21.
- Recurring statewide figures still being cited in coverage: at least 38 Texas data centers have received minor-source permits for on-site power since 2024, enabling more than 2,100 backup diesel generators statewide; proposed facilities statewide could emit more than 287 million tons of greenhouse gases annually.
- Pacifico Energy's GW Ranch project in Pecos County remains the largest air permit ever granted in the U.S. — 7.65 GW of gas-fired generation for hyperscale data centers — cited by advocates as evidence TCEQ's minor-source review process is being outpaced by the industry it regulates.

**Today's Signal:** Today's EPA hearing is the live event to track — its outcome will determine how much advance notice DFW-area communities get on future gas- and diesel-generator permits.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**Fort Worth and Denton Still Pointing to Later Dates — Nothing New Filed Today**

- No new DFW-area county rezoning filing or vote surfaced dated specifically to today.
- Fort Worth's zoning fight remains set for August 11, when City Council reconvenes from summer break to take up the data-center ordinance the Zoning Commission denied 7-4 on July 8. A Fort Worth Report piece published yesterday (July 21) examined whether the proposed moratorium can produce a meaningful long-term solution or just delay the underlying zoning fight. Five council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) remain on record backing a temporary moratorium; Mayor Parker and three others have not signed on.
- Denton's push remains in an earlier stage than Fort Worth's: City Council directed staff in early June to explore a possible moratorium, and a July 16 community town hall kept pressure on the issue, but no formal council vote date has been set.
- Earlier flashpoints remain unresolved in the background: Parker County's pushback over Black Mountain's 2,000-plus-acre purchase, and Hood County's conditional approval of the 2,100-acre "Comanche Circle" proposal near Glen Rose.

**Today's Signal:** With Fort Worth's council vote three weeks out and Denton still without a set date, today is a quiet day on DFW land use — yesterday's Fort Worth Report analysis is worth a read for context heading into August.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**No New Dated Deal Today — Navarro County and Pecos Projects Remain the Marquee Stories**

- No new on-site generation or gas-plant announcement dated specifically to today surfaced.
- Hedgehog USA's July 21 agreement with Idemitsu (Item 2) remains the most recent power-first signal: a 600 MW campus built to run on natural gas burned on-site, explicitly avoiding the multi-year ERCOT interconnection queue.
- Equipment bottlenecks continue to be cited as the structural driver: switchgear reportedly sold out through 2028, and substation transformer lead times stretched to roughly 160 weeks and rising.
- The Chevron-Microsoft 20-year power purchase agreement for a dedicated natural-gas plant near Pecos (roughly 2.5 GW initially, ~$7 billion, targeting 2027) and Microsoft's separate ~2.67 GW co-located gas plant for Project Kilby remain the marquee statewide power-first deals. The pending EPA minor-source rollback (Item 3, hearing today) would directly ease the permitting path for the diesel and gas behind-the-meter fleets DFW-region data centers rely on to satisfy Senate Bill 6's backup-generation requirement for facilities of 75 MW or more.

**Today's Signal:** Today's EPA hearing (Item 3) is the cross-cutting story for this section too — its outcome affects how easily operators can permit the on-site generation power-first strategies depend on.
