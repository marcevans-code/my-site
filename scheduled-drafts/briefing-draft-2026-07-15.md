## ⚡ 1. ERCOT & Grid Signals

**Today Is a Batch Zero Milestone Date**

- July 15 is itself a Batch Zero deadline: projects seeking energization by December 31, 2027 must demonstrate development progress today to stay in the process, per ERCOT's interconnection framework.
- This follows the July 10 deadline for large-load entities to submit technical studies, attestations, and forms to their interconnecting TSP/DSP, and precedes the July 24 deadline for TSPs to submit the full package (Load Information Form, Dynamic Stability Study Form, and related attestations) to ERCOT itself.
- A final deficiency-cure deadline of August 31 closes out the Batch Zero submission process; independent analysts estimate roughly 55 large-load projects totaling 21.7 GW are positioned to make the cut.
- ERCOT's large-load interconnection queue remains near 438-439 GW systemwide, with about 89% of that demand attributed to data centers — still roughly one-third of all U.S. generating capacity and about five times Texas's current peak demand record.

**Today's Signal:** Watch for confirmation from ERCOT or large-load developers that today's development-progress showing was met — a miss here could bump projects out of the Batch Zero cohort before the August 7 classification notice.

## 🏗️ 2. Data Center Construction & Corporate Activity

**Plug Power Sells Graham, Texas Site to Stream Data Centers**

- Plug Power announced July 13 a definitive agreement to sell its Graham, Texas project — land plus 164 MW of grid interconnection assets — to Stream Data Centers for up to $76.5 million ($50 million at closing, up to $26.5 million tied to final confirmed load capacity). Closing is expected on or about July 31. Plug is retaining the substation and interconnection assets along with a repurchase right. Graham sits in Young County, just northwest of the core DFW metro.
- At OpenAI's Stargate campus in Abilene, reporting describes roughly 5,000 workers pushing around the clock to finish the 1.2 GW campus — outside the immediate DFW footprint but a bellwether for the pace of hyperscale build-out statewide.
- Fermi America's Project Matador near Amarillo remains a story of stalled momentum: the company says an "initial phase" of construction is complete, but satellite tracking has not shown building starts, and the company has cited a pending final Clean Air Permit ruling as a hold-up.
- No new dated construction, land-acquisition, or interconnection filing specific to the core DFW counties (Tarrant, Denton, Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Wise, Parker, Hood) surfaced today.

**Today's Signal:** The Plug Power/Stream deal is a reminder that land-plus-substation packages, not just raw acreage, are becoming the tradable unit in this market — worth watching for similar deals nearer the DFW core.

## 🌿 3. Environmental & Air-Permit Activity (TCEQ)

**EPA Proposes Cutting the Public Out of Minor-Source Permitting — Directly Hits Data Center Generators**

- On July 7, the Trump administration's EPA proposed eliminating public participation and transparency requirements for "minor source" air permits — a category that covers the diesel and gas generators many Texas data centers rely on. Sierra Club's Lone Star Chapter went public with its objection July 9.
- Context cited in that release: since 2024, at least 38 Texas data centers have received "minor" permits for on-site power sources, and the state has approved more than 2,100 backup diesel generators — critics call it a "small first, big later" permitting tactic that avoids the scrutiny larger permits would trigger. The Vantage data center near San Antonio, cited as an example, is set to run 80-plus diesel generators.
- EPA has scheduled a public hearing on the proposal for July 22 (pre-registration due July 19); written comments are due August 21.
- No new dated TCEQ draft permit or public-comment notice specific to Collin, Ellis, Johnson, Denton, Wise, Tarrant, Parker, or Hood counties turned up today. The DFW area's existing 10-county serious ozone nonattainment designation (attainment deadline August 2, 2027) remains the backdrop against which any new gas-plant permit near DFW will be judged.

**Today's Signal:** The EPA's minor-source rollback is the biggest environmental-policy lever in play right now — if finalized, it would remove one of the few public-comment checkpoints on the diesel/gas generator fleets clustering around DFW-area data centers.

## 🏛️ 4. Land-Use & Zoning Developments

**Fort Worth Ordinance in Council's Hands • Denton Town Hall Tomorrow Night**

- Fort Worth's Zoning Commission denied the city's proposed data-center ordinance 7-4 on July 8 (250-foot home setbacks, industrial-only siting, crypto-mining ban). City Council takes it up again August 4, with a final vote expected August 11.
- Five Fort Worth council members (Beck, Flores, Hall, Martinez, Nettles) have publicly committed to proposing a temporary moratorium on new data-center applications at that same August meeting.
- In Denton, DSA Denton and the Revolutionary Front hold a town hall tomorrow, Thursday, July 16, at 6:30 p.m. at Emily Fowler Central Library to organize around a possible local moratorium or ban; Denton's City Council is separately expected to take up its own moratorium proposal in August. Organizers are citing a regional count of 86 planned data centers in North Texas, 56 in Central Texas, and 45 in West Texas.
- No new DFW-area rezoning filings or county moratorium votes turned up dated specifically to today; the Plug Power/Stream Graham, Texas transaction (Item 2) is the closest land-use-adjacent news, though it sits just outside the core DFW county list.

**Today's Signal:** Tomorrow's Denton town hall is the most immediate date on the calendar — the turnout and tone there will likely shape what Denton's council brings to its own August moratorium discussion.

## 🔌 5. Power-First Strategies & On-Site Generation

**Chevron-Microsoft Permian Deal Advances Toward 2027**

- Chevron and Microsoft have a 20-year power purchase agreement for a natural-gas-fired plant near Pecos in the Permian Basin, developed with investment firm Engine No. 1, sized at roughly 2.5 GW initially (ramping toward 2.67 GW and potentially 5 GW), at an estimated cost near $7 billion, targeting operation by 2027. It's Chevron's first dedicated data-center power asset — a Permian Basin project, not DFW, but the clearest current example of the "power-first" behind-the-meter model.
- The EPA's proposed minor-source permitting rollback (Item 3) would make it procedurally easier for on-site generation — including gas and diesel behind-the-meter fleets — to be sited with less public review, reinforcing the broader power-first trend.
- No new dated DFW-specific on-site generation or gas+battery hybrid announcement surfaced today.

**Today's Signal:** The regulatory and commercial threads are converging — a major permitting rollback (Item 3) and a marquee $7 billion behind-the-meter deal (this item) both point toward faster, less-scrutinized on-site power builds as the industry's default strategy.
